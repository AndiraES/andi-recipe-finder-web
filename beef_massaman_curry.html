    About Beef Massaman Curry :
    Beef Massaman Curry is a Thai dish known for its rich and aromatic flavors. It features tender beef cooked in a creamy coconut milk sauce with a blend of spices like cinnamon, cardamom, and nutmeg. Potatoes, onions, and peanuts are often included, and it's typically served with jasmine rice.

    Ingredients :

    • 500g beef, thinly sliced
    • 2 potatoes, peeled and cubed
    • 1 can (400ml) coconut milk
    • 2-3 tablespoons Massaman curry paste (store-bought or homemade)
    • 1 onion, sliced
    • 2-3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, minced
    • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
    • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
    • 1 tablespoon palm sugar (or brown sugar)
    • 3-4 cardamom pods
    • 2-3 bay leaves
    • 1 cup chicken or beef broth
    • Vegetable oil
    • Salt, to taste
    • Roasted peanuts (optional, for garnish)
    • Fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)
    Instructions:
    1. Heat some vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the Massaman curry paste and stir-fry for a minute until fragrant.
    2. Add the minced garlic, ginger, and sliced onion to the pot. Cook for another 2-3 minutes until the onions are soft and translucent.
    3. Add the thinly sliced beef to the pot. Cook until the beef is browned on all sides.
    4. Pour in the coconut milk and chicken or beef broth. Stir well to combine.
    5. Add the cubed potatoes, cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, bay leaves, tamarind paste, fish sauce, and palm sugar to the pot. Stir to combine all the ingredients.
    6. Bring the curry to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer gently for about 1 to 1.5 hours, or until the beef is tender and the potatoes are cooked through. Stir occasionally.
    7. Once the beef and potatoes are cooked, taste the curry and adjust the seasoning with more fish sauce, palm sugar, or salt if needed.
    8. Serve the Beef Massaman Curry hot, garnished with roasted peanuts and fresh cilantro if desired. Enjoy with steamed rice or Thai jasmine rice.

    This recipe serves about 4 people. Feel free to adjust the ingredients and seasonings according to your taste preferences. Enjoy your homemade Beef Massaman Curry!
