    About Chili Crab :
    Chili Crab is a quintessential dish in Singaporean cuisine, featuring succulent crab cooked in a tangy, spicy chili sauce. It's a beloved symbol of the city-state's culinary heritage and a must-try for visitors exploring Singapore's vibrant food scene.

    Ingredients:

    • 2 whole mud crabs (about 1-1.5 kg each), cleaned and cut into pieces
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 5 cloves garlic, minced
    • 2 inches ginger, minced
    • 3-4 red chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to spice preference)
    • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
    • 2 tablespoons chili sauce or sambal oelek
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
    • 1 tablespoon sugar
    • 1 cup chicken or seafood broth
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • Salt, to taste
    • Spring onions, chopped (for garnish)
    • Coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
    Instructions:
    1. Heat vegetable oil in a large wok or deep pan over medium heat. Add chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and red chilies. Stir-fry until fragrant and the onions are translucent
    2. Stir in the tomato paste, chili sauce, soy sauce, oyster sauce, and sugar. Mix well to combine all the ingredients.
    3. Pour in the chicken or seafood broth and bring the mixture to a simmer.
    4. Gently add the crab pieces to the wok, stirring to coat them evenly with the sauce. Cover the wok and let the crab simmer in the sauce for about 8-10 minutes, or until the crab shells turn red and the meat is cooked through.
    5. Taste the sauce and adjust the seasoning with salt or sugar if needed.
    6. Once the crab is cooked, transfer it to a serving dish. Garnish with chopped spring onions and coriander leaves.
    7. Serve the Chili Crab hot with steamed rice or mantou buns to soak up the delicious sauce.

    Enjoy your homemade Singaporean Chili Crab, a flavorful delight that captures the essence of Singapore's vibrant food culture!
