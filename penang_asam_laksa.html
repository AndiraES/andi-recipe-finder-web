    Malaysia's Food

    About Penang Asam Laksa :
    Penang Asam Laksa is a famous Malaysian noodle soup dish originating from the state of Penang. It's known for its complex flavors, combining sour, savory, and spicy elements in a single bowl. The soup base is made from mackerel fish, tamarind, lemongrass, and various spices, giving it a tangy and aromatic profile. The soup is poured over rice noodles and topped with a variety of fresh ingredients such as cucumber, onions, pineapple, mint leaves, and torch ginger flower. Penang Asam Laksa is celebrated for its rich cultural heritage, reflecting the multicultural influences of Malaysian cuisine. It's a beloved dish enjoyed by locals and visitors alike, often ranked among the world's best foods.

    Ingredients :

      For the Soup :

    • 200g of mackerel fish (or any firm, white fish)
    • 1 liter of water
    • 2 stalks of lemongrass, bruised
    • 4-5 pieces of dried tamarind slices (asam gelugor)
    • 2 tablespoons of tamarind paste
    • 3-4 pieces of dried torch ginger flower (bunga kantan), sliced thinly
    • 1 tablespoon of shrimp paste (belacan)
    • Salt and sugar to taste

      • For the spice paste (rempah) :

    • 10-12 dried red chilies, soaked in hot water and deseeded
    • 5 shallots, peeled
    • 3 cloves of garlic, peeled
    • 1 inch of fresh turmeric, peeled (or 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder)
    • 1 inch of galangal, peeled
    • 1 stalk of lemongrass, white part only
    • 1 tablespoon of shrimp paste (belacan)
    • 1 tablespoon of toasted belacan powder (optional)

      • Other ingredients :

    • 200g of thick rice noodles (rice vermicelli)
    • 1 cucumber, julienned
    • 1 onion, thinly sliced
    • 1 small pineapple, julienned
    • A handful of mint leaves
    • A handful of laksa leaves (daun kesum)
    • Torch ginger flower (bunga kantan), thinly sliced
    • Vietnamese coriander (daun kesom) (optional)
    • 2-3 red chilies, thinly sliced
    • 2-3 calamansi limes, halved
    Instructions:
    1. Prepare the fish broth :
      In a pot, bring the water to a boil. Add in the mackerel fish and lemongrass stalks.
      Boil for about 10-15 minutes until the fish is cooked through. Remove the fish from the broth and set aside to cool.
      Strain the broth to remove any impurities. Return the broth to the pot.
    2. Prepare the spice paste :
      Blend all the ingredients for the spice paste in a food processor or blender until it forms a smooth paste.
    3. Cook the spice paste :
      Heat some oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the blended spice paste and shrimp paste.
      Fry the paste until fragrant and the oil begins to separate from the paste, about 5-7 minutes.
    4. Assemble the soup :
      Add the cooked spice paste, dried tamarind slices, tamarind paste, torch ginger flower, and shrimp paste to the fish broth.
      Season the soup with salt and sugar to taste. Simmer for another 10-15 minutes.
    5. Prepare the noodles and toppings :
      Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
      Flake the cooked mackerel fish into small pieces, discarding any bones.
      Prepare all the toppings - cucumber, onion, pineapple, mint leaves, laksa leaves, torch ginger flower, red chilies, and calamansi limes.
    6. Assemble the laksa :
      To serve, place a portion of rice noodles in a bowl. Ladle the hot soup over the noodles.
      Top with the flaked fish and desired toppings.
      Squeeze some calamansi lime juice over the laksa before serving.

    Enjoy your delicious Penang Asam Laksa! Adjust the spiciness and tanginess according to your taste preference.
