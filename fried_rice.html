    About Fried Rice :
    Indonesian fried rice, known as "Nasi Goreng," is a popular dish that reflects the rich and diverse culinary heritage of Indonesia. It typically consists of stir-fried pre-cooked rice with a mix of ingredients such as shallots, garlic, tamarind, chili, soy sauce, and shrimp paste, along with various proteins like chicken, shrimp, or tofu. Nasi Goreng is often served with a fried egg on top and garnished with crispy shallots or sliced cucumber. It's a flavorful and aromatic dish enjoyed throughout Indonesia and appreciated worldwide for its unique blend of sweet, savory, and spicy flavors.

    Ingredients:

    • 3 cups cooked white rice (preferably cooked a day before and chilled)
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 small onion, finely chopped
    • 1-2 red chilies, finely chopped (adjust according to your spice preference)
    • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
    • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers, etc.), diced
    • 2 tablespoons sweet soy sauce (kecap manis)
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 teaspoon shrimp paste (optional)
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Spring onions, chopped (for garnish)
    • Fried shallots (for garnish)
    • Cucumber slices and tomato wedges (for serving)
    Instructions:
    1. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat.
    2. Add minced garlic and chopped onion to the skillet. Saut√© until fragrant and onions are translucent.
    3. Add chopped red chilies and mixed vegetables to the skillet. Stir-fry for a few minutes until vegetables are slightly tender.
    4. Push the vegetables to one side of the skillet and pour the beaten eggs onto the other side. Allow the eggs to cook for a minute or two until they start to set, then scramble them until fully cooked.
    5. Add the chilled cooked rice to the skillet, breaking up any clumps with a spatula.
    6. Pour the sweet soy sauce, regular soy sauce, and shrimp paste (if using) over the rice. Stir well to combine all ingredients evenly.
    7. Cook the fried rice, stirring occasionally, until heated through and slightly crispy, about 5-7 minutes.
    8. Taste the fried rice and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper if necessary.
    9. Once the fried rice is cooked to your liking, remove it from the heat and transfer it to serving plates.
    10. Garnish the fried rice with chopped spring onions and fried shallots.
    11. Serve the Indonesian fried rice hot, accompanied by cucumber slices and tomato wedges.

    Enjoy your homemade Nasi Goreng!
