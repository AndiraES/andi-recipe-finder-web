    • Pastel de Nata
    Portugal's Food

    About Pastel de Nata :
    Pastel de nata is a traditional Portuguese pastry known for its flaky crust and creamy custard filling. It's typically sprinkled with cinnamon or powdered sugar. These delightful treats originated in Lisbon in the 18th century and are now enjoyed throughout Portugal and around the world. They're often served with coffee and are a beloved symbol of Portuguese culinary culture.

    Ingredients:

      For the Pastry :

    • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed if frozen

      • For the Custard Filling :

    • 2 cups whole milk
    • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
    • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
    • 3 large egg yolks
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
    • Zest of 1/2 lemon (optional)

      • For Dusting :

    • Powdered sugar
    • Ground cinnamon (optional)
    Instructions:
    1. Preheat the Oven :
      • Preheat your oven to 220°C (425°F).
    2. Prepare the Pastry :
      • Roll out the puff pastry sheet on a lightly floured surface until it is about 1/8 inch thick.
      • Cut the pastry into squares about 3x3 inches in size.
      • Press each square into the cups of a muffin tin, making sure the pastry comes up the sides of each cup.
    3. Make the Custard Filling :
      • In a saucepan, whisk together the milk, sugar, and flour over medium heat until the mixture thickens and begins to bubble.
      • In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks until smooth.
      • Gradually pour the hot milk mixture into the egg yolks, whisking constantly to prevent curdling.
      • Return the mixture to the saucepan and cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until it thickens to a custard consistency.
      • Remove the custard from the heat and stir in the vanilla extract and lemon zest (if using).
    4. Fill the Pastries :
      • Spoon the custard filling into the prepared pastry cups, filling them about 2/3 full.
    5. Bake :
      • Place the muffin tin in the preheated oven and bake the pastries for about 15-20 minutes, or until the pastry is golden brown and the custard is set.
    6. Serve :
      • Remove the pastries from the oven and let them cool in the tin for a few minutes.
      • Carefully remove the pastries from the tin and transfer them to a wire rack to cool completely
      • Dust the pastries with powdered sugar and ground cinnamon before serving, if desired.

    Enjoy your homemade pastel de nata! They're best served warm or at room temperature.
