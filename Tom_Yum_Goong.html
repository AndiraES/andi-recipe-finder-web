    About Tom Yum Goong :
    Tom Yum Goong is a quintessential Thai dish, a hot and sour soup featuring shrimp (Goong). It's infused with aromatic herbs like lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and chili peppers. The broth is typically seasoned with fish sauce, lime juice, and sometimes coconut milk, creating a flavorful balance of spicy, sour, salty, and sometimes creamy flavors. It's often served with rice and is known for its vibrant and refreshing taste.

    Ingredients:

    • 10-12 large shrimp (peeled and deveined)
    • 4 cups of chicken or seafood broth
    • 2 stalks of lemongrass (sliced)
    • 4-5 slices of galangal (or ginger)
    • 4-5 kaffir lime leaves
    • 2-3 Thai bird's eye chili peppers (adjust according to spice preference)
    • 200g mushrooms (straw mushrooms or button mushrooms)
    • 2 tomatoes (quartered)
    • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
    • 3-4 tablespoons lime juice
    • 1 tablespoon sugar (optional)
    • Handful of cilantro leaves (for garnish)
    Instructions:
    1. In a pot, bring the chicken or seafood broth to a boil.
    2. Add lemongrass, galangal, kaffir lime leaves, and chili peppers to the broth. Let it simmer for 5-10 minutes to infuse the flavors.
    3. Add mushrooms and tomatoes to the pot. Cook for another 5 minutes until the mushrooms are tender.
    4. Add shrimp to the pot and cook until they turn pink and opaque, which usually takes about 2-3 minutes.
    5. Season the soup with fish sauce, lime juice, and sugar (if using). Adjust the seasoning according to your taste preference.
    6. Remove the pot from heat and ladle the soup into serving bowls.
    7. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves.
    8. Serve hot with steamed rice.

    Enjoy your homemade Tom Yum Goong! Adjust the spice level and ingredients according to your preference.
