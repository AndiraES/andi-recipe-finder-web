    South Africa's Food

    About Bunny Chow :
    Bunny chow is a South African fast food dish consisting of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry. It originated in the Indian community of Durban, South Africa, in the 1940s, where it was initially sold as a cheap and portable meal for Indian laborers. Today, bunny chow is enjoyed by people of all backgrounds across South Africa and is often served with a side of pickles or sambals.

    Ingredients:

      For the Curry :

    • 500g (1 lb) chicken, beef, lamb, or vegetables (cut into bite-sized pieces)
    • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
    • 1 onion, finely chopped
    • 2 garlic cloves, minced
    • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
    • 2 tomatoes, chopped
    • 2 tablespoons curry powder
    • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
    • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
    • 1 teaspoon turmeric
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 cup water or chicken/beef stock
    • Fresh cilantro (coriander) leaves for garnish (optional)

      • For Serving :

    • Loaf(s) of bread, such as a round white bread loaf or unsliced bread rolls
    Instructions:
    1. Prepare the Curry :
      • Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot or skillet over medium heat.
      • Add the chopped onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
      • Add the minced garlic and ginger, and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
      • Add the chopped tomatoes, curry powder, ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric, salt, and pepper to the pot. Stir well to combine.
      • Add the chicken, beef, lamb, or vegetables to the pot and cook until browned.
      • Pour in the water or stock and bring the mixture to a simmer. Cover and cook for about 20-30 minutes, or until the meat is tender and the flavors have melded together. If using vegetables, cook until they are tender.
      • Taste and adjust seasoning if needed. Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves if desired.
    2. Prepare the Bread :
      • While the curry is cooking, prepare the bread. Cut a large round loaf of bread or unsliced bread rolls in half horizontally.
      • Hollow out the center of each bread half, leaving a thick shell of bread around the edges. Reserve the bread you've removed to serve alongside the bunny chow, if desired.
    3. Assemble the Bunny Chow :
      • Once the curry is ready, spoon it generously into the hollowed-out bread halves.
      • Serve immediately, with the reserved bread on the side for dipping.

    Enjoy your homemade South African bunny chow! It's a delicious and hearty dish that's perfect for sharing with friends and family.
